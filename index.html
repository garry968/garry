Garrison Keane GIS Portfolio

In this semester, we learnt how to solve problems in a GIS framework.
We used ArcGIS pro, ArcGIS Developer and codepen.io and environment to solve the problem. We also used various datasets including census and election data in this course to solve problems.
Below are my three favourite maps that I made in this course.

First Map

Homicide Density Map

My first favorite map was the map I made in the fourth lab. The lab taught us to manipulate uncooperative data to created insights. In this module, I learned how to combine, summarize, and change attribute tables by using joins and calculating new fields. The assignment for this week was to describe the distribution of homicide density in Chicago and to compare the spatial patterns of homicides, Airbnb listings, and Airbnb prices in Chicago.

From the map districts 11 and 15 have the highest number of homicides per square mile in Chicago(1.5-2.5). Most(19 of 25) police districts report the lowest homicide cases per square mile in Chicago(None- 0.5)

Second Map

Election Map of Philadelphia

My second favourite map was a map I made during the second mastery assignment. We created choropleth maps using data from Miami County and the state of Philadelphia. The assignment taught me projecting data, joining tables to spatial layers, calculating geometry, and creating a new field from other fields.
This favorite map is an election map, that I created in the third part of the assignment. The map is anelection map of Philadelphia that shows how each county voted for the president in 2020

The map uses red color for Trump and blue color for Biden for counties that voted in favor of each candidate. The map also has a saturation of color depending on how the favored candidate won.

Third Map

Water pollution in Michigan

My third favorite map is map from the third mastery assignment, that shows areas where people are at risk of drinking poisonous tap water in Flint, Michigan.
I used intersection, clipping, join, buffer, and map-making map-making tools to find these areas.
